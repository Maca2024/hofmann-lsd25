001 — THE PROBLEM CHILD

LSD-25

Lysergic acid diethylamide · C₂₀H₂₅N₃O

Synthesized from ergot alkaloids by Albert Hofmann on November 16, 1938, at Sandoz Laboratories in Basel. Its profound psychoactive properties were discovered accidentally on April 16, 1943 — and confirmed three days later on the legendary Bicycle Day, April 19, when Hofmann intentionally ingested 250μg and rode his bicycle home through the dissolving streets of Basel, becoming the first human to experience a full LSD journey.

002 — THE SPIRIT MOLECULE

DMT

N,N-Dimethyltryptamine · C₁₂H₁₆N₂

The most powerful naturally occurring psychedelic known to science. Found endogenously in the human brain, in hundreds of plant species, and in the venomous secretions of the Bufo alvarius toad. The Amazonian brew Ayahuasca combines DMT-containing plants with MAO inhibitors, creating a 4-6 hour oral journey through what many describe as contact with non-human intelligence, geometric cathedrals of light, and the fundamental architecture of reality itself.

003 — TEONANÁCATL

Psilocybin

4-Phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine · C₁₂H₁₇N₂O₄P

Sacred mushrooms have been used for at least 7,000 years — depicted in Tassili cave paintings and Mesoamerican codices. Mazatec curandera María Sabina shared the velada ceremony with R. Gordon Wasson in 1955. Albert Hofmann himself isolated and synthesized psilocybin in 1958. Today, clinical trials at Johns Hopkins, Imperial College, and NYU show remarkable efficacy for treatment-resistant depression, end-of-life anxiety, and addiction — with a single dose producing lasting personality changes toward openness.

004 — THE ANCIENT HEALER

Cannabis

Δ⁹-Tetrahydrocannabinol · C₂₁H₃₀O₂

Humanity's oldest cultivated psychoactive plant, with archaeological evidence dating to 12,000 years ago in Central Asia. The Scythians burned it in ritual tents. Chinese Emperor Shen Nung documented its medicine in 2737 BCE. THC and CBD interact with the endocannabinoid system — a network of receptors that regulate pain, mood, appetite, and neuroplasticity. Over 140 cannabinoids work in an entourage effect, creating a pharmacological symphony unique in all of nature.

005 — THE DESERT TEACHER

Mescaline

3,4,5-Trimethoxyphenethylamine · C₁₁H₁₇NO₃

The sacred medicine of the peyote cactus (Lophophora williamsii) and San Pedro (Echinopsis pachanoi), used by indigenous peoples for over 5,700 years. The first psychedelic to be isolated (Arthur Heffter, 1897) and synthesized (Ernst Späth, 1919). Aldous Huxley's 1954 mescaline experience inspired "The Doors of Perception," giving name to both a cultural revolution and Jim Morrison's band. Its geometric visions are legendary — form constants that mirror the mathematical structure of consciousness itself.

006 — THE ROOT OF TRUTH

Ibogaine

12-Methoxyibogamine · C₂₀H₂₆N₂O

Extracted from the root bark of the West African shrub Tabernanthe iboga, central to the Bwiti tradition of Gabon. Ibogaine is unique among psychedelics: a single flood dose can interrupt opioid, cocaine, and alcohol addiction by resetting the brain's reward pathways. It produces a 24-36 hour visionary state often described as a panoramic life review — every memory examined without emotional charge. Research into its anti-addictive properties continues to accelerate worldwide.
